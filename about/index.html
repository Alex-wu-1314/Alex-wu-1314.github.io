Hi, I’m Alex Wu — a software engineer deeply passionate about low-level systems, hardware-aware software, and real-time communication technologies.

My technical focus spans several interconnected domains:

  • 3GPP Layer 2 Protocol Processing: Deep experience in designing and optimizing high-throughput, low-latency L2 processing frameworks for 4G/5G NR—covering MAC, RLC, and PDCP layers, with emphasis on lock-free data structures, batch processing, and NUMA-aware scheduling.
  • CPU Architecture & Performance Optimization: Exploring instruction set design, cache hierarchies, branch prediction, and microarchitectural bottlenecks.
  • WebRTC & Real-Time Communication: Implementing and troubleshooting full WebRTC stacks — from signaling and ICE/STUN/TURN to jitter buffer tuning and adaptive bitrate control.

I believe the best engineering happens at the intersection of hardware and software. This blog is where I document my experiments, share insights from the lab, and reflect on the evolving landscape of computing and communication.
